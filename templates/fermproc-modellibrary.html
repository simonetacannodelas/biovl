Saccharomyces cerevisiae (Aerobic)

Responsable of beer and wine. Maybe one of the most appreciated microorganisms in the universe.

This beautiful yeast has a unique physiology and it is one of the most used wee beasties (biological systems) as host for genetical manipulations. Also, it has a long history of safety use and consumption, and lack of production of toxins, so therefore, most strains of S.cerevisiae are considered as safe (GRAS).

Moreover in the industry, it is highly appreciated due to its ability of growth under aerobic and anaerobic conditions. Naturally, Saccharomyces cerevisiae is used for the production of yeast or ethanol.

Mannheimia succiniciproducens (Anaerobic)

One of the wee beasties used in the production of succinic acid in large scale.

Succinic acid is used in agricultural, food and pharmaceutical industries.Succinate has been place in the top 12 pf platform chemicals from biomass. It is the precursor to some polyesters and biodegradable polymers. Furthermore, succinci acid is also an important safe food adictive as an acidity regulator; as well as in the pharmaceutical products. In pharmaceutics, it is also a fundamental intermediate product.

Historically, succinic acid was obtained form amber by distillation and has thus been know as spirit of amber. It global production is estimated at 16,000 to 30,000 tons a year, with an annual growth rate of 10%. It is a great example of the transition from a swith to a fermetation production
